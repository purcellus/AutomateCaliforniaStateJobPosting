Department Information

Mission of the Franchise Tax Board: Our mission is to provide the services and information to help taxpayers file accurate and timely tax returns and pay the proper amount owed. To accomplish this mission, we develop knowledgeable and engaged employees, administer and enforce the law with fairness and integrity, and responsibly manage the resources allocated to us.

Department Website: http://www.ftb.ca.gov

Job Description and Duties

If you enjoy working in a dynamic team environment where you can apply your critical thinking, analytical, and technical skills to help Franchise Tax Board (FTB) maintain its external taxpayer services applications including the new MyFTB taxpayer self-service portal, then we have a great opportunity for you. We are looking for an individual who is motivated and interested in performing quality assurance testing on the department's complex web-based applications that provide taxpayers and other stake holders 24x7 self-service options. You will work collaboratively with technical and nontechnical resources across the department to ensure applications meet the quality expectations of our department and taxpayers. This includes performing testing that ensures applications are as accessible as possible while maintaining strict security of taxpayer data. You will be responsible for developing and maintaining test plans, test scripts, test data conditions, test executions, results, findings, and summaries. You will get to work in a fast paced and challenging yet rewarding environment that requires flexibility and honors team work.

Additional positions may become available.

RPA#: 1617-00798

Special Requirements

  • The position(s) requires incumbents pass a Background Investigation prior to being hired. The Background Investigation may include Criminal Background, Fingerprinting, and a review of any governmental records.

**IMPORTANT NOTICE** As required by the department's rules of conduct, all employees of the Franchise Tax Board (FTB) must be in compliance with the California Revenue and Taxation Code. During the background investigation portion of the hiring process, FTB will review return information to verify California state income tax compliance.  Any failure to comply with the California Revenue and Taxation Code may prevent you from being hired at FTB. You must verify your filing status by calling 1-800-852-5711 to resolve any potential tax compliance issues, prior to submitting your application and seeking employment with FTB. All applicants not currently employed with the Franchise Tax Board will be subject to a pre-employment background investigation.  The investigation will consist of completion of a pre-employment questionnaire, fingerprinting, and an inquiry to the Department of Justice to disclose criminal records. We comply with the Americans with Disabilities Act. Persons with hearing or speech impairments, call TTY/TDD (800) 735-2929.

Application Instructions

Completed applications and all required documents must be received by the Final Filing Date in order to be considered.

Final Filing Date: 10/5/2016

Who May Apply

Completed applications and all required documents must be received or postmarked by the Final Filing Date in order to be considered. Individuals who are currently in the classification, eligible for lateral transfer, eligible for reinstatement, eligible for a Training and Development assignment, have SROA and/or Surplus eligibility, or have list eligibility (or are in the process of obtaining list eligibility). SROA and Surplus candidates may attach surplus letters to their applications. Applicants must meet any Minimum Qualifications stated in the Classification Specification(s). Applications will be screened and only the most qualified applicants will be selected for an interview.

How To Apply

Complete Application Packages (Applications and any applicable or required documents) must be submitted electronically through your CalCareer account at www.jobs.ca.gov.

Required Application Documents

Please submit the following items with your application. Applicants who do not submit the required items timely may not be considered for this job:

  • State Examination/Employment Application STD Form 678. All Experience and Education relating to the Minimum Qualifications listed on the Classification Specification should be included to demonstrate how you meet the Minimum Qualifications for the position.
  • Other -
    • Cover letter 
  • Supplemental Application - In order to be considered for this position, interested applicants must submit a supplemental application. Your response must be no more than two type-written pages in length, using 12-point font, 1 inch margins and include your first and last name in the upper right-hand corner of each page. Respond to the following supplemental statement: 

    Describe a time in which you were assigned to a time-sensitive project with no prior knowledge or experience with that workload. What was the project? What did you do? What steps did you take to complete the project? What was the outcome?
  • Resume is required and must be included.
Applicants requiring reasonable accommodations for the hiring interview process must request the necessary accommodations if scheduled for a hiring interview. The request should be made at the time of contact to schedule the interview. Questions regarding reasonable accommodations may be directed to the EEO contact listed on this job posting.

Desirable Qualifications

In addition to evaluating each candidate's relative ability, as demonstrated by quality and breadth of experience, the following factors will provide the basis for competitively evaluating each candidate:
  • Knowledge of the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC) and system testing principles, methodologies, techniques and tools
  • Knowledge in accessibility compliance analysis and testing of interactive web applications and outputs (reports, notices, PDF) to Federal/State government codes, requirements, industry standards, and best practices
  • Experience in quality assurance or system testing phases (e.g., test preparation, test execution, test resolution/summary)
  • Knowledge of different scopes of testing including: exploratory/ad-hoc testing, system testing, regression testing, and defect confirmation testing
  • Knowledge of federal and state web standards and mandates (e.g., security, accessibility, and usability)
  • Demonstrated ability to take initiative, multi task, handle changing priorities, meet critical deadlines, and use tact and good judgment
  • Strong analytical skills and ability to think logically and creatively
  • Knowledge of testing tools (e.g., IBM Rational Quality Manager)
  • Efficient and accurate in completing work
  • Excellent interpersonal skills, including customer service and communication skills for interacting with business and technical staff
  • Knowledge of tax administration programs and processes similar to FTB's business programs and/or knowledge of FTB's Internet applications, external taxpayer folder and related services
