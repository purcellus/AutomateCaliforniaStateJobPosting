Department Information

The California Department of Education (CDE) oversees the state's diverse and dynamic public school system, which is responsible for the education of more than seven million children and young adults in more than 9,000 schools. The CDE and the State Superintendent of Public Instruction are responsible for enforcing education law and regulations; and for continuing to reform and improve public elementary school programs, secondary school programs, adult education, some preschool programs, and child care programs. The CDE's mission is to provide a world-class education for all students, from early childhood to adulthood. The CDE serves our state by innovating and collaborating with educators, schools, parents, and community partners, preparing students to live, work, and thrive in a highly connected world.
Department Website: http://www.cde.ca.gov

Job Description and Duties

Under the general supervision of the Education Administrator in the CALPADS/CBEDS/CDS Operations Office (CCCOO) of the Educational Data Management Division, and working cooperatively with program area staff and supervisors throughout the Department, the Associate Information Systems Analyst (Specialist) provides technical and data analysis support to the California Longitudinal Pupil Achievement Data System (CALPADS), the California Basic Educational Data System (CBEDS), and the County-District-School (CDS) Administration.

 

  • Maintain MS Access databases in support of office data collections; perform data validation and trend analysis, develop ad-hoc queries, reports, and graphic user interfaces using MS Access and MS SQL Server.
  • Conduct functional, usability, acceptance, and beta testing in support of office data collections including: the development and use of industry standard test plans, development of test cases, development of test data files, execution and documentation of application testing, and reporting on testing status.
  • Provide technical support to the division, office, and local educational agencies (LEAs).
  • Support the administration of local CALPADS administration processes and the charter school submission statuses.
  • Provide technical liaison activities between the unit and other divisions and governmental agencies.
  • Develop and maintain requirements documentation to meet current reporting requirements and system user needs for the CBEDS Online Reporting Application (ORA) and Online Public Update for Schools (OPUS) CDS application.
  • Provide technical support for the office ticket tracking system (FootPrints) workspaces.
  • Assist the office lead in the design, development, testing, and implementation of the Footprints database.
  • Develop and maintain CBEDS and CDS Web-based materials.
  • Work with the Data Reporting Office to coordinate and verify release of CBEDS downloadable data files.
  • Participate in staff meetings; keep supervisors apprised of project status; assist staff as needed; perform other job-related duties as required.
