Department Information

The California Department of Education (CDE) oversees the state's diverse and dynamic public school system, which is responsible for the education of more than seven million children and young adults in more than 9,000 schools. The CDE and the State Superintendent of Public Instruction are responsible for enforcing education law and regulations; and for continuing to reform and improve public elementary school programs, secondary school programs, adult education, some preschool programs, and child care programs. The CDE's mission is to provide a world-class education for all students, from early childhood to adulthood. The CDE serves our state by innovating and collaborating with educators, schools, parents, and community partners, preparing students to live, work, and thrive in a highly connected world.
Department Website: http://www.cde.ca.gov

Job Description and Duties

Under the direction of the Data Processing Manager IV, the Associate Information Systems Analyst, as a member of the Web Services Unit, assists in the development, review, and maintenance of the Departments Intranet and Internet Web sites. The incumbent serves as the primary analyst responsible for reviewing Web content submitted for posting to the Intranet and Internet Web sites, and for providing user support to Web Contributors. The incumbent performs with a high level of initiative and independent responsibility to meet project objectives and timelines. Duties include, but are not limited to the following:

 

  • Daily Web Activities Performs day-to-day routine Web activities and operations including customer support, Web page reviews and approvals, analyzing customer requests for Web services, uploading web pages to the Departments Intranet and Internet Web sites, and resolving technical issues and requests that are submitted by the Departments Web coordinators.

  • Web Page Creation and Maintenance Uses the Departments templates to create and maintain Web pages on the Departments Intranet and Internet Web sites as directed by management.

  • Web Contributor Trainer Acts as the assistant trainer for division Web coordinators, division Web developers, and Web content providers; provides overview of Web server configuration; creates training materials; provides training on Web-related software and Web applications; provides training on Web site standards and accessibility to ensure standards are implemented throughout the Department.

  • Web Application Development Assists in the development and maintenance of Web-based applications using standard Department development tools.

  • Policy, Standards and Processes Proposes new and/or improved Webrelated policies, standards and processes. Reference http://www.cde.ca.gov/re/di/ws/webstandards.asp

Desirable Qualifications

In addition to evaluating each candidate's relative ability, as demonstrated by quality and breadth of experience, the following factors will provide the basis for competitively evaluating each candidate:

  • Working experience providing end-user technical support.

  • Ability to teach technical content in a hands-on, computer lab environment.

  • Basic understanding of dynamic, database-drive Web technologies.
