Job Description and Duties

Under the supervision of the Systems Software Specialist (SSS) II Supervisor, the Associate Systems Software Specialist (ASSS) performs at the full journey level in the design, analysis, installation, and oversight of projects directly related to the Departments Enterprise Information Systems (EIS) operating system environment. The incumbent is responsible for research, installation, configuration, security, maintenance, troubleshooting, backup, recovery, and support of all EIS applications.  The incumbent is responsible for resolving software and configuration problems that arise in support of the email and messaging environment, directory services, secure e-mail systems, file systems, identity management, storage management systems, mobile device management systems, and associated applications. The ASSSs role as a project lead and technical advisor requires knowledge in the software and hardware associated to this environment in order to consult with all Departmental entities. Occasional travel is required.

Who May Apply

Completed applications and all required documents must be received or postmarked by the Final Filing Date in order to be considered. Individuals who are currently in the classification, eligible for lateral transfer, eligible for reinstatement, eligible for a Training and Development assignment, have SROA and/or Surplus eligibility, or have list eligibility (or are in the process of obtaining list eligibility). SROA and Surplus candidates may attach surplus letters to their applications. Applicants must meet any Minimum Qualifications stated in the Classification Specification(s). Applications will be screened and only the most qualified applicants will be selected for an interview.

Desirable Qualifications

In addition to evaluating each candidate's relative ability, as demonstrated by quality and breadth of experience, the following factors will provide the basis for competitively evaluating each candidate:
  • Sound problem-solving skills and decision-making abilities.
  • Ability to work cooperatively and communicate effectively with all levels of staff as well as management and vendors.
  • Attack problems with a "can do" attitude.
  • Deep technical knowledge and experience in directory services: mainly LDAP and Active Directory; Identity Management; e-mail and Archiving systems; and operating systems as VMWare and Microsoft. 
  • Ability to contribute innovative solutions.
  • Knowledge and principals of Mobile Device Management technologies.
  • Willingness and ability to learn new concepts and technologies.
  • Must be able to work under pressure and meet deadlines.
  • Willingness to work outside of normal business hours.
  • Possess ability to work as a team player.
  • Effective written, verbal, and interpersonal skills and the ability to think logically.
  • Familiarity and knowledge of e-mail, directory services, file systems, identity management and storage management system.
  • Familiarity and knowledge of project management principals.
  • Ability to multitask and delegate.
